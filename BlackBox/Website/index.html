Frequently Asked Questions

Does BlackBox send my data anywhere?

No. Every scan runs entirely on your Mac. BlackBox makes zero network requests. There is no telemetry, no analytics, and no account required.

Is the free scan really free?

Yes. The full 4-module audit — photo GPS, ghost files, sensitive strings, and telemetry — is completely free with no time limit. You only pay if you want to take action on the findings.

What does "neutralize" actually do?

It strips GPS metadata from photos (rewriting files with kCFNull), overwrites deleted file remnants with 3-pass random data + fcntl(F_FULLFSYNC), and adds tracking domains to your hosts file. All originals are backed up to the AES-256 encrypted Shadow Vault first.

What if something goes wrong?

Every file is backed up to the encrypted Shadow Vault before any changes are made. You have 48 hours to use the Emergency Rewind feature to restore anything to its original state.

Why does it need Full Disk Access?

macOS sandboxes apps by default. Full Disk Access lets BlackBox scan your photo library, caches, and documents. Without it, the audit can only see a limited subset of files. You can revoke access at any time in System Settings.

What macOS versions are supported?

BlackBox requires macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later. It's built with SwiftUI and Metal, and runs natively on Apple Silicon and Intel Macs.
